Hi. I'm Matthew Horrigan, communication scholar. I research media production cultures.

My teaching connects the technical skills of writing, podcasting, and video with theories of labor and management.

I have guided more than a thousand students from the global community through seminars, labs, lectures, and online learning. In the age of GPT, my students adapt to new technology while keeping their own voices.

In an earlier artistic career, I was a sound designer and computer programmer. I wrote code for art installations and musical performances. I treat computing as a form of literacy, and believe that modern life demands not just fluency with, but understanding of, the inner workings that make our public images.
